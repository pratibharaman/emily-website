F.A.Q.

What is Occupational Therapy (OT)?
OT is a form of therapy that aims to improve a person's ability to engage in their meaningful activities of daily life. OT supports clients in a variety of settings, including but not limited to schools, hospitals, sensory gyms, rehabilitation centers, and clients' homes. My OT services aim to support children in their engagement in their daily activities, including but not limited to academic tasks, self-care tasks, and play, with an aim of increasing meaningful activity participation, developing confidence and competence, and greater independence. Sessions may address fine motor skills, visual motor skills, and sensory processing and regulation abilities, activity modifications, environmental modifications, and caregiver education.
